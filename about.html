About The K-Beauty Data Desk

Captured Olive Young aggregate review data, with scoped topic counts, metadata flags, and explicit limits.

What this site is

Korea's Olive Young rankings provide aggregate signals from skincare audiences. Each article here takes one product from a captured ranking snapshot and turns those aggregate ratings and polls, plus a capped sample of anonymous topic counts and metadata flags when available, into plain English. The output distinguishes ratings, poll representation, topic labels, and buying checks instead of treating them as product outcomes.

Where the numbers come from

The average rating, review count, star distribution, and displayed skin-type, concern, and gentleness polls come from the captured Olive Young Korea product page. When deep analysis is available, the pipeline temporarily examines a capped sample of up to 500 helpful review records, discards their text, and retains only anonymous topic counts and metadata-flag shares. Those flags do not verify buyer identity, intent, satisfaction, or causation. I do not test products on my own skin, and I say so in every article.

Check me

Every post includes a footer link to the product's Olive Young page so readers can compare the current display with the dated snapshot; the source page can change after capture. Prices are quoted only as dated anchors with market and date labels, and current listings must be checked before purchase. When data is missing, articles say "no data" instead of printing a zero, and a small captured review base is flagged as limited context rather than dressed up as a settled verdict.

About the illustrations

Article visuals are synthetic and labeled as such: data cards and charts drawn by code from the product's own aggregate numbers, plus generic format illustrations. They illustrate a format category, not the actual product, application, or skin result. You will not find before-and-after pictures, faces, breakout or irritation photos, or anything built from a reviewer's own photo here. I do not test products on my own skin, so it would be dishonest to show you a result, and a picture of a rare side effect would make a 1% number look like a 100% one. The reaction data stays a chart, drawn to scale.

Who runs this

One person and a data pipeline. I stay unnamed for now, which I know costs some trust. Captured storefront aggregates are source-linked; capped-sample counts and currency conversions are pipeline outputs with their basis and limits stated. Judge the site on that record.

What I never do

  • Copy, quote, or translate any individual user's review, name, or photo. Only aggregate numbers.
  • Invent ingredient percentages. I report only captured label order, with the caveat that label rules and low-level ingredient ordering can limit what position means.
  • Make medical or clinical claims. These are cosmetics.
  • Accept payment for coverage. Products are chosen by one rule: they top the sales ranking.

Affiliate disclosure

Store links currently contain no affiliate tracking ID; this site earns no commission from them. Link configuration never changes the numbers, and an article telling you to skip a product is a perfectly good outcome here.
