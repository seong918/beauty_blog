About The K-Beauty Data Desk

I read all the reviews so you don't have to. Honest, data-backed takes on Korea's best-selling skincare.

What this site is

Korea's Olive Young rankings show what tens of thousands of very picky skincare shoppers actually keep buying, but all of that review data is locked away in Korean. Each article here takes one current best-seller and turns its full review record into plain English: what buyers love, what they complain about, who keeps repurchasing, and who should skip it.

Where the numbers come from

Every statistic is Olive Young Korea's own aggregate review data for that product: the average rating, the review count, the star distribution, and the skin-type and satisfaction polls buyers fill in. For most articles I also read a large sample of the most helpful reviews line by line and report patterns as percentages. I do not test products on my own skin, and I say so in every article.

What I never do

  • Copy, quote, or translate any individual user's review, name, or photo. Only aggregate numbers.
  • Invent ingredient percentages. Ingredient lists are ordered by amount, so I only ever talk about an ingredient's position on the label.
  • Make medical or clinical claims. These are cosmetics.
  • Accept payment for coverage. Products are chosen by one rule: they top the sales ranking.

Affiliate disclosure

Store links in posts are affiliate links. If you buy through one, I may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. That never changes the numbers, and an article telling you to skip a product is a perfectly good outcome here.
