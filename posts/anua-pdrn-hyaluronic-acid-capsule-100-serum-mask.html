What's actually in the bottle (I read all 75 ingredients)

The actives are mostly the marketing. The moisturizing is the product. The label wants you chasing the buzzy stuff, so here's the honest split. The PDRN is genuinely in there, not a fairy-dusted token. But Hydrolyzed collagen, ceramides, and Copper peptide are riding right at the bottom of the list, which is in-there-for-the-label, not for-your-face. (An ingredient list runs in amount order, never exact percentages, so I won't invent a number.) What's actually doing the hydrating is the unglamorous part: workhorses like hyaluronic acid and panthenol. A good hydrator, with the trendy actives along for the ride. Texture-wise, it's silicone-based (that's the slip and the dewy finish) and no drying alcohol. One real safety stop: the PDRN is Sodium DNA, derived from salmon, so a fish or shellfish allergy means skip it. That's an allergen, not a preference. Two quick heads-ups, the kind that are either your whole problem or nothing at all. There's no added fragrance (a genuine plus), but it does carry calming plant extracts (soybean, neem leaf, and ivy-gourd extract), and those can still set off reactive or eczema-prone skin, so patch-test if that's you. And if you get fungal acne, this isn't your bottle: the probiotic ferment, light MCT oil, and soybean in here are the kind of thing malassezia feeds on. The rest is supporting cast: thickeners, a preservative system, pH balancers. I read every line, and there's nothing scary and nothing thrilling hiding down there. Want the exact label positions? How to use it (without wasting it)

Routine order trips people up, so here's the no-nonsense version for this format:
