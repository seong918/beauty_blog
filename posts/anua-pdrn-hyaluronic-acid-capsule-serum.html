What's actually in the bottle (I read all 75 ingredients)

The actives are mostly the marketing. The moisturizing is the actual product. The label wants you fixated on the buzzy stuff, so here's the honest split. The PDRN is genuinely in there, not a fairy-dusted token. But Hydrolyzed collagen, ceramides, and Copper peptide are parked right at the bottom of the list, which is there-for-the-label, not for-your-face. (Ingredient lists run in order of amount, never exact percentages, so I won't invent a number.) The thing doing the hydrating is the unglamorous part: workhorses like hyaluronic acid and panthenol. A solid hydrator, with the trendy actives riding shotgun. On texture, it's silicone-based (that's the slip and the dewy finish) with no drying alcohol. One genuine safety stop: the PDRN is Sodium DNA, derived from salmon, so a fish or shellfish allergy means skip it. That's an allergen, not a preference. Two more heads-ups, the kind that are either your entire problem or a total non-issue. No added fragrance (a real plus), but it does carry calming plant extracts (soybean, neem leaf, and ivy-gourd extract), and those can still trip up reactive or eczema-prone skin, so patch-test if that's you. And if fungal acne is your thing, this isn't your bottle: the probiotic ferment, light MCT oil, and soybean in here are exactly what malassezia feeds on. The rest is supporting cast: thickeners, a preservative system, pH balancers. I read every line, and nothing scary and nothing thrilling is hiding down there. Want the exact label positions? How to use it (without wasting it)

Routine order trips people up, so here's the no-nonsense version for this format:

  1. Cleanse first, then move thinnest to thickest texture.
  2. Press in 2–3 drops on clean, slightly damp skin before moisturizer.
  3. Give it a minute to sink in before the next layer.
  4. Use AM and/or PM; seal with moisturizer (and SPF in the morning).
